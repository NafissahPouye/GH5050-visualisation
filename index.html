Sex, Gender and COVID-19: Tracking national differences in COVID-19 cases, illness and death among women and men

Findings of the Global Health 50/50 Sex-Disaggregated COVID-19 Data Tracker

From the first reports of the novel coronavirus, COVID-19, there has been consistent evidence of a gendered impact on health outcomes.
In many settings, women appear to be slightly more likely to be diagnosed with COVID-19, which may in part be due to the fact that women account for the majority of health care workers around the world. At the same time, there is a consistent pattern of higher death rates recorded among men compared to women. This pattern seems to be repeated in nearly every countriesâ€™ reporting.
The Global Health 50/50 COVID-19 Data Tracker has been collating sex-disaggregated COVID-19 data reported by national governments since March 2020. This includes data on confirmed cases, hospitalizations, ICU admissions and deaths. We are also tracking data on cases and deaths by age, as well as infections among healthcare workers.
Many countries do not report COVID-19 cases, deaths and other data disaggregated by sex. These gaps are reflected in the missing data below.
For more information and to download the data, visit https://globalhealth5050.org/covid19/
